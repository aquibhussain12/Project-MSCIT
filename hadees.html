Hadiths are the sayings, actions, and approvals (or disapprovals) of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) in Islam. They serve as a significant source of guidance for Muslims, providing practical examples of how to implement the teachings of the Quran in daily life. The two primary sources of Islamic guidance are the Quran and the Hadith.
The hadiths were transmitted orally and later compiled into collections by scholars. Some well-known collections include Sahih Bukhari, Sahih Muslim, Sunan Abu Dawood, Sunan at-Tirmidhi, Sunan an-Nasa'i, Sunan Ibn Majah, and Muwatta Imam Malik.
Here's an example of a hadith:
The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said: "The strong is not the one who overcomes the people by his strength, but the strong is the one who controls himself while in anger." (Sahih al-Bukhari)
This hadith emphasizes the importance of self-control and patience, highlighting that true strength lies in mastering one's emotions and reactions.
It's essential to note that not all hadiths are considered equally authentic, and scholars have categorized them based on their chains of transmission and content. The most authentic are classified as Sahih (sound) or Hasan (good), while others may be deemed weak or fabricated.

Sahih Bukhari

"Sahih Bukhari" is one of the most authentic collections of sayings and actions of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) in Islam. It consists of over 7,000 hadiths (sayings and actions of the Prophet) categorized into various chapters. The compilation aims to provide a reliable source of guidance for Muslims in matters of faith, practice, and conduct.

Access Sahih Bukhari

Sahih Muslim

"Sahih Muslim" is highly regarded collection of authentic hadiths in Islam, compiled by Imam Muslim. It consists of over 7,000 narrations, organized into chapters covering various aspects of Islamic teachings. Like Sahih Bukhari, Sahih Muslim is considered one of the most reliable sources for understanding the traditions and teachings of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

Access Sahih Muslim

Sunan an-Nasa`i

Sunan an-Nasa'i is a collection of hadiths compiled by Imam Ahmad an-Nasa'i, featuring around 5,000 narrations. Organized into chapters, it covers diverse aspects of Islamic jurisprudence, rituals, and conduct. While not as widely known as Sahih Bukhari or Sahih Muslim, Sunan an-Nasa'i holds significance in the study of hadith and is considered one of the six major Sunni hadith collections.

Access Sunan an-Nasa`i

Sunan Abi Dawud

Sunan Abi Dawud is a collection of hadiths compiled by Imam Abu Dawud. It contains over 4,800 narrations categorized into chapters, covering a wide range of topics related to Islamic practices, rituals, and legal rulings. While not as extensive as some other hadith collections, Sunan Abi Dawud is highly regarded and studied for its authenticity and valuable insights into the teachings of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

Access Sunan Abi Dawud

Jami` at-Tirmidhi

Jami at-Tirmidhi is a collection of hadiths compiled by Imam al-Tirmidhi, containing around 3,956 narrations. It is organized into chapters covering various aspects of Islamic jurisprudence, ethics, and guidance. Considered one of the six major hadith collections in Sunni Islam, Jami at-Tirmidhi is esteemed for its efforts to authenticate and organize hadiths while providing valuable context and explanations.

Access Jami` at-Tirmidhi

Sunan Ibn Majah

Sunan Ibn Majah is a collection of hadiths compiled by Imam Ibn Majah, featuring around 4,341 narrations. It covers diverse topics such as rituals, ethics, and legal rulings, organized into chapters. While not as widely studied as some other major hadith collections, Sunan Ibn Majah holds significance in providing additional insights into the traditions of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) and is one of the six major Sunni hadith compilations.
